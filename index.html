Academic Work

Research papers and projects from graduate study at NCCU's Graduate Institute of Global Communication and Technology Innovation.

Polymedia Survival in the Margins: Digital Survival Strategies of Taiwanese and Thai LGBTQ+ Youth and Reflections on Technology Governance
Research Proposal · NCCU GCIT
An explanatory sequential mixed-methods study examining how LGBTQ+ youth in Taiwan and Thailand develop cross-platform "polymedia" survival strategies — navigating algorithmic surveillance on mainstream social media while turning to generative AI as an alternative emotional sanctuary.
Proposal
2025
Between Visibility and Strategy: LGBTQ+ Representation in South Korean Popular Dramas (2007–2022)
Course Paper · NCCU GCIT
A mixed-methods study combining content analysis and case study, examining LGBTQ+ representation across the 100 most globally popular K-dramas. In-depth analysis of Itaewon Class and Move to Heaven reveals both the potential and limitations of queer inclusion in Korean media storytelling.
Paper
2025
Between Hesitation and Implementation: Investigating Taiwan High School Teachers' Attitudes Toward AI in the Classroom and Designing a Practical Collaboration Template
Course Project · NCCU GCIT · with Chen, Yu-Sheng · supervised by Prof. Sunny Xun Liu
A qualitative study using semi-structured interviews with high school humanities teachers in Taiwan, examining their attitudes, anxieties, and strategies around AI integration. Findings informed the design of an AI-assisted teaching starter kit — TeachFlow — addressing differentiated learning needs across the pre-, during-, and post-class instructional cycle.

教案 發想 應用

將傳播研究與 HCI 觀點轉化為實際可用的教學設計——媒體識讀、數位公民素養，與跨領域應用。

媒體識讀不應該只停留在 fact check。
Media literacy is more than fact-checking.

媒體識讀是每個人的課題——數位暴力、平台權力結構與演算法設計的傷害，青少年、一般使用者、邊緣族群都無法倖免。這些教案相信，透過在學教育更能夠防範這些傷害於未然。希望 bridge 學術研究與教學現場，讓老師和學生一起建立對數位世界更清醒的眼光。

Media literacy is everyone's challenge — digital violence, platform power structures, and algorithmic design can harm youth, everyday users, and marginalized communities alike. These lesson designs believe that school-based education is one of the most powerful places to build prevention. They aim to bridge academic research and the classroom, helping teachers and students develop a clearer, more critical eye for the digital world.
